Anukaran

Ankuran, a Hindi word which means “to germinate”. When you sow a seed, it does not grow into a tree overnight. It need nutrients from the soil, water and sunlight to grow. A seed transforms into a sapling, which is weak, tender, and needs care. As it keeps growing, it grows in girth. The bark turns brown and strengthens. After a considerable amount of time, a cluster of molecules which was once a seed is now a behemoth, a tree which is self-sufficient and provides invaluable services to the animal kingdom selflessly. An idea develops in a similar way. When it is conceptualised, it is still weak. It needs time and thought to develop into something tangible. The more it is shared with other people, the bigger it gets and the stronger it grows. An idea is a seed, which germinates in a person’s mind. Giving it time and thought is necessary to ensure that the idea becomes a reality. At TEDxNUV, we have specially curated talks which would excite your thought process, making your brain the ideal place for an idea to germinate.
